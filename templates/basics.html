Astrology Basics

A beginner-friendly guide to reading the app without getting lost in jargon

What To Read First

Astrology can become confusing when everything looks equally important. It is not. In this app, the best reading order is: first the D1 birth chart, second the Ascendant and its ruler, third the Moon, fourth the main planetary placements and house rulers, fifth yogas and strengths, sixth timing through dashas, and finally transits and BCC/KP manifestation shifts. If something appears later, it usually refines the earlier layers instead of replacing them.

D1 / Rashi Chart

This is the main birth chart. Think of it as the master blueprint. If you only looked at one chart, this would be the one.

Ascendant

The Ascendant is the sign rising at birth. It describes the person carrying the whole chart: body, direction, instinct, and how life begins to unfold.

Ascendant Lord

This is the ruler of the Ascendant sign. Many astrologers treat it as one of the most important planets because it shows how the native actually operates in life.

Moon

The Moon shows the lived mind: emotions, habits, inner experience, and what a person feels day to day. It is also central to dasha timing.

Sun

The Sun shows vitality, confidence, authority, purpose, and the drive to express oneself.

Sign

A sign shows the style or flavor of a planet.

House

A house shows the life area where something happens: relationships, career, money, home, spirituality, and so on.

Nakshatra

A nakshatra is a lunar mansion. It adds a deeper psychological script or motive behind the planet.

Pada

A pada is one quarter of a nakshatra. It gives a finer detail, like a micro-tone inside the nakshatra.

House Lord

Each house has a ruling planet. Astrologers often judge a house by looking at its ruler first, because the ruler carries that house's results into another part of the chart.

Dasha

Dasha means planetary time period. This is one of the most important timing tools in Vedic astrology.

Mahadasha

The main chapter of life. It sets the broader tone for years.

Antardasha

The sub-period inside the Mahadasha. It explains why the same major period changes flavor over time.

Pratyantardasha

A smaller trigger inside the Antardasha. It is useful for the current active wave.

Transit / Gochar

This shows where planets are moving now. Transits usually trigger what the birth chart and dasha already made possible.

Jaimini Karakas

These are role-significators based on planetary degree. In this app, they use the 7 classical planets only.

Atmakaraka

The planet with the highest degree. It points to the soul-pressure, deepest lesson, or life purpose theme.

Amatyakaraka

The second-highest degree planet. It is strongly tied to work, implementation, career direction, and how the soul purpose becomes action.

Darakaraka

The partnership significator in Jaimini astrology. It helps describe what kind of relationship karma the person carries.

Important Note

Rahu and Ketu are not used as Jaimini Chara Karakas in this app.

Yoga

A yoga is a special planetary combination. Some yogas are powerful, but they should support the chart basics rather than replace them.

Shadbala

A strength score for planets. Useful, but not more important than the actual chart structure.

Aspects

Aspects show which planets are influencing each other across the chart.

Conjunction

A conjunction means planets are together in the same sign/house area, blending their energies.

D9 / Navamsha

A divisional chart used for inner maturity, marriage, and the deeper condition of planets. It confirms the D1; it does not replace it.

D10 / Dashamsha

A divisional chart used mainly for career and public work.

Which Vargas Matter Most

For most people, D9 and D10 are the most useful extra charts. Other vargas are more specialized and should only be used carefully, especially when birth time is uncertain.

BCC / KP Bhava Chalit

This is the manifestation layer. The D1 chart shows the natal setup, while BCC helps show where results land in lived experience, especially in dashas.

How To Think About It

A simple rule: D1 tells you what the planet is; BCC tells you where that result tends to show up in real life.
